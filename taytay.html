Taytay, Rizal

Taytay, formally known as the Municipality of Taytay is a 1st class municipality in the province of Rizal, Philippines. It is also known as the Garments Capital of the Philippines and Woodworks Capital of the Philippines. Taytay is the country's second most populated municipality, with a 38.8-kilometer land area and about 400 thousand residents. Below are some places to visit:

Taytay Tiangge

Taytay Tiangge is made up of various clothing tents that sell relatively inexpensive, fashionable, and high-quality apparel. Many of the garments sold at the tiangge are manufactured by the Taytayeos, popularly known as the "Garments Capital of the Philippines." Taytay Capital Tiangge, Bagpi Garment Center, CME Tiangge, and IGPAI Garment Center are among the notable tents in the tiangge.

Taytay Tiangge

Christ the King Parish, often known as "Church in the Sky," is a church with a panoramic view in Taytay, Rizal. Guests and visitors may overlook the entire town of Taytay and Angono up to Laguna Lake from an outstanding perspective right outside the church. The parish was built in 1984 and the first parish created under the Diocese of Antipolo.

J-la's Epalog

J-La's Epalog is a new popular go-to food store in Taytay, Rizal. With over 30 years in the industry, J-La's epalog claims to be the "home of the original epalog." Epalog comes from the words "hepa log," hepa, and itlog. According to the owner, it is named that way because of how similar the color of the egg is to those people who have hepa, or hepatitis, but they later changed the name to epalog to stop the negative connotation.
